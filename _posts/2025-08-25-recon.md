## Google Dorking Commands

#### Basics
> üîç `site:example.com`  
  ‚Üí shows results for the domain example.com only  

> üîç `site:*.example.com`  
  ‚Üí all subdomains of example.com (e.g. mysite.example.com)  

#### For Login Pages
> üîç `site:example.com inurl:admin`  

> üîç `site:*.example.com inurl:admin/login`  
  ‚Üí ("admin login") use quotes for multiword phrases  

> üîç `site:*.example.com intitle:login/admin`  
  ‚Üí ("login" / "admin" / "login page") use quotes for exact words  

#### Directory Listing
> üîç `site:*.example.com intitle:"Index of /"`  
  ‚Üí very useful and powerful  

> üîç `site:*.example.com intitle:"index of" "parent directory"`  
  ‚Üí finds pages with `<title>Index of</title>` containing "parent directory"  

> üîç `site:*.example.com inurl:ftp`  
  ‚Üí old but sometimes used for files/backups  

> üîç `site:*.example.com inurl:wp-content`  
  ‚Üí for WordPress sites  

#### Finding Files
> üîç `site:example.com ext:xls OR ext:csv OR ext:doc`  

> üîç `site:example.com (ext:pdf OR filetype:pdf)`  
  ‚Üí ext:pdf is more precise than filetype:pdf (other: txt, robots.txt, sitemap, docx, xlsx, log, xml)  

> üîç `site:example.com filetype:env`  
  ‚Üí try env, ini, sql, bak for sensitive info
